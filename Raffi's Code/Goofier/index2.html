Jacques de Molay

About Jacques de Molay

Jacques de Molay, born in 1243 in Molay, France, was the last Grand Master of the Knights Templar, serving from 1292 until the order's dissolution.

Under his leadership, the Knights Templar faced increasing pressure and scrutiny from the King of France, Philip IV, leading to their eventual arrest and persecution.

Jacques de Molay was famously executed on March 19, 1314, in Paris, where he was burned at the stake.

His legacy as the final Grand Master of the Knights Templar has endured through the centuries, with his defiance and martyrdom inspiring many.
