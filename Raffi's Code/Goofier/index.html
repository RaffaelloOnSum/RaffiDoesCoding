The Knights Templar

About the Knights Templar

The Knights Templar, originally known as the Poor Fellow-Soldiers of Christ and of the Temple of Solomon, were a medieval Christian military order formed in the early 12th century.

They were among the most skilled fighting units of the Crusades, recognized by their distinctive white mantles adorned with a red cross.

The Knights Templar played a significant role in Christian finance and banking, and they were known for their vast network of financial infrastructure across Europe.

Despite their prominence, the Knights Templar faced controversy and persecution, leading to their dissolution in the early 14th century.
